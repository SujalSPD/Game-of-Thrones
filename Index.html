The Series

Game of Thrones is an American fantasy drama television series created by David Benioff and D.B. Weiss for HBO. It is an adaptation of A Song of Ice and Fire, a series of fantasy novels by George R.R. Martin, the first of which is A Game of Thrones.

Set on the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, the series follows several interweaving plot lines and a large ensemble cast. The first story arc follows a dynastic conflict among competing claimants for succession to the Iron Throne of the Seven Kingdoms, with other noble families fighting for independence from the throne.

The second covers the attempts to reclaim the throne by the exiled last scion of the realm's deposed ruling dynasty. The third follows the longstanding brotherhood charged with defending the realm against the ancient threats of the fierce peoples and legendary creatures that lie far north and an impending winter that threatens the realm.

Filmed across Northern Ireland, Malta, Croatia, Iceland, and Spain, the series premiered on HBO in the United States on April 17, 2011, and concluded on May 19, 2019, with 73 episodes broadcast over eight seasons.

Main Characters

Daenerys Targaryen

Mother of Dragons, Breaker of Chains

The last surviving member of House Targaryen, Daenerys begins as a frightened girl sold into marriage to Khal Drogo. Through tragedy and fire, she hatches three dragons and rises to become a powerful queen determined to reclaim the Iron Throne. Her journey from exile to conqueror is marked by liberation of slaves and an increasingly ruthless pursuit of power.

House Targaryen

Jon Snow

The White Wolf, King in the North

Raised as the bastard son of Ned Stark, Jon joins the Night's Watch to escape his status as an outsider. He rises to become Lord Commander, forges an alliance with the wildlings, and is resurrected after betrayal. Later revealed as Aegon Targaryen, the true heir to the Iron Throne, he must navigate between duty, honor, and love.

House Stark / Targaryen

Arya Stark

No One, The Night King Slayer

The youngest Stark daughter rejects traditional female roles, training in swordplay and vowing revenge against those who wronged her family. After fleeing King's Landing, she trains with the Faceless Men in Braavos, learning the art of assassination. She returns to Winterfell a deadly warrior and defeats the Night King in the Battle of Winterfell.

House Stark

Sansa Stark

Lady of Winterfell, Queen in the North

Initially a naive girl dreaming of romance and royalty, Sansa endures brutal captivity in King's Landing, abuse by Ramsay Bolton, and political machinations by Littlefinger. She emerges as a wise and capable leader, ultimately becoming Queen in the North after securing independence from the Seven Kingdoms.

House Stark

Cersei Lannister

Queen of the Seven Kingdoms

Twin sister to Jaime and wife of Robert Baratheon, Cersei is ruthless, ambitious, and fiercely protective of her children. After the death of her children and her humiliation by the Faith Militant, she seizes the Iron Throne through wildfire destruction. Her reign is marked by paranoia and desperate defense against Daenerys's invasion.

House Lannister

Jaime Lannister

The Kingslayer

Known as the Kingslayer for murdering the Mad King Aerys II, Jaime begins as an arrogant knight but undergoes significant transformation after losing his hand. His complex relationship with Brienne of Tarth reveals his honor, while his love for Cersei ultimately leads to his redemption and death in the ruins of King's Landing.

House Lannister

Tyrion Lannister

Hand of the King, The Imp

A dwarf despised by his father and sister, Tyrion survives through wit, intelligence, and strategic brilliance. He serves as Hand to Joffrey and later Daenerys, proving to be one of the most capable administrators in Westeros. His knowledge of history and politics makes him invaluable, eventually becoming Hand to King Bran the Broken.

House Lannister

The Night King

The Great Other, Death Incarnate

The supreme leader and first of the White Walkers, created by the Children of the Forest thousands of years ago as a weapon against the First Men. He commands the army of the dead and seeks to erase the memory of mankind from the world. He breaches the Wall with an undead Viserion and falls at the Battle of Winterfell.

World Building

🏰 The Seven Kingdoms

Westeros is divided into seven distinct regions, each with its own culture and history. The North, ruled by House Stark from Winterfell, is cold and ancient. The Vale is mountainous and impregnable. The Riverlands, Westerlands, Reach, Stormlands, and Dorne each offer unique landscapes and political dynamics, united under the Iron Throne but often seeking independence.

🐉 Dragons & Magic

Dragons were the source of Targaryen power for centuries. Balerion the Black Dread, Vhagar, and Meraxes conquered Westeros. After dying out, they were reborn with Daenerys's three dragons: Drogon, Rhaegal, and Viserion. Magic in this world is rare but real—shadow binders, wargs, greenseers, and the resurrection powers of the Lord of Light all exist beyond the Wall and in distant Essos.

❄️ The Wall & Beyond

An immense wall of ice stretching 300 miles along the northern border of the Seven Kingdoms, guarded by the Night's Watch. Beyond lies the haunted forest and the Land of Always Winter, home to the Free Folk (wildlings) and the ancient enemy: the White Walkers. The Wall was built with magic and ice 8,000 years ago after the Long Night.

⚔️ The Great Houses

House Stark (Winter is Coming), House Lannister (Hear Me Roar), House Targaryen (Fire and Blood), House Baratheon (Ours is the Fury), House Greyjoy (We Do Not Sow), House Tyrell (Growing Strong), and House Martell (Unbowed, Unbent, Unbroken) form the major powers. Their sigils, words, and ancestral weapons like Valyrian steel swords define their identities.

🙏 Religion & Faith

The Faith of the Seven dominates the south, worshipping the Father, Mother, Warrior, Smith, Maiden, Crone, and Stranger. The North keeps to the Old Gods of the Forest, worshipped before the Andal invasion. The Iron Islands follow the Drowned God, while R'hllor the Lord of Light gains followers through his priests like Melisandre, wielding the power of resurrection.

⚖️ The Iron Throne

Forged from the swords of Aegon the Conqueror's defeated enemies using dragonfire, the Iron Throne sits in the Red Keep of King's Landing. It is uncomfortable, dangerous, and symbolic—designed so no ruler should ever sit easy. The pursuit of this throne drives the War of the Five Kings and the conflicts that shape the fate of Westeros.

Legendary Facts

01

Most Expensive TV Show Ever

The final season cost $15 million per episode, with a total budget of $90 million for just six episodes. The "Battle of Winterfell" took 55 consecutive nights of shooting in freezing conditions.

02

Record-Breaking Emmy Winner

Game of Thrones won 59 Primetime Emmy Awards out of 161 nominations, the most by any drama series in history. It holds the record for most Emmys won by a series in a single season (12 in Season 5).

03

Global Phenomenon

The series averaged over 30 million viewers per episode in its final season across all platforms. It was broadcast in over 170 countries and translated into more than 40 languages.

04

The Unseen Pilot

The original pilot episode was almost entirely reshot. Only two scenes from the original remain in the series: the Stark family finding the direwolf pups and part of the feast at Winterfell.

05

Real Locations

Filming took place across Northern Ireland (The North), Dubrovnik, Croatia (King's Landing), Malta (early King's Landing), Iceland (beyond the Wall), and Spain (Dorne and Dragonstone).

06

The Red Wedding Reaction

The infamous "Red Wedding" episode caused such an emotional reaction that author George R.R. Martin stopped going to fan conventions for a time, as he couldn't handle the grief from fans over the deaths of Robb and Catelyn Stark.

07

Valyrian Steel

The legendary swords like Longclaw, Oathkeeper, and Widow's Wail are made of Valyrian steel, forged with magic and dragonfire in Old Valyria. Only a few smiths in the world can reforge it.

08

The Hodor Reveal

The tragic origin of Hodor's name ("Hold the door") was suggested by Kristian Nairn (the actor who played Hodor) years before it was revealed in the show, though he didn't know the context.
